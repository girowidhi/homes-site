Buying Property

What are the initial steps to buying a home in Kenya? expand_more
The process typically starts with identifying a property and conducting a site visit. Once satisfied, you perform an official search at the Ministry of Lands. Following this, an offer letter is issued, and a Sale Agreement is drafted by the seller's advocate. A deposit (usually 10-20%) is then paid into an escrow account.
What legal fees should I expect during a purchase? expand_more
Buyers should budget for Stamp Duty (4% for urban properties, 2% for rural), Legal Fees (standardized by the Advocates Remuneration Order), and Valuation Fees. Additionally, there may be small administrative costs for land searches and registration.
Can non-Kenyan citizens buy land in Kenya? expand_more
Yes, foreigners can own land in Kenya, but under a leasehold tenure (typically 99 years) rather than freehold. Foreigners are also restricted from owning agricultural land unless a special waiver is granted.

Real Estate Investment

Why invest in Nairobi?

Nairobi remains a regional hub with high demand for luxury apartments and commercial spaces, offering rental yields between 6-8% in prime areas.

Off-plan vs. Ready?

Off-plan properties offer lower entry prices and capital appreciation during construction, while ready properties provide immediate rental income.

How does TobillionHomes vet new developments? expand_more
We conduct rigorous due diligence on developers, including past project delivery, financial stability, and verification of all statutory approvals (NEMA, NCA, and County approvals).
